Notable Medical Illustrators

There have been a lot of medical illustrators and not all of them have received due credit for their work.

This page shares some of the most notable illustrators and provides a few examples of their work and their contributions to the field.

Leonardo da Vinci

One of the most renowned medical illustrators from the Renaissance period. His anatomical work remained unpublished and relatively unknown until 1760 and resides in the British Royal Library at Windsor today.

(1452-1519)

Nicolas Henri Jacob

Known for illustrations completed with anatomist Jean-Baptiste Marc Bougery, in his Traite complet de l’anatomy de l’homme.

(1782-1871)

Frank H. Netter

American surgeon and medical illustrator, known for producing Netter’s Atlas of Human Anatomy, published 1989.

(1906-1991)

David Bolinksy

Former lead medical illustrator at Yale; cofounder of XVIVO, which produced The Inner Life of the Cell; known for 3D computer graphic animations of molecular mechanisms

(1952-present)

Markus Voll

German graphic artist known for his illustrations in a variety of publications, including medical books from Thieme publishers.

(1961-present)

Alissa Eckert & Dan Higgins

Known for creating the CDC's digital illustration of COVID-19. Both have created other digital illustrations of biological molecules, as well.

(2019)
